☀back in la: home sweet home☀

June 8, 2016

First things first: happiest of 22nd birthdays to my brother Tim! A little about Tim: he's my older brother and my only sibling, a software engineer currently working at Amazon in Irvine, and in general a pretty nice guy (except to me) and also the most sarcastic. Not-so-secret secret: he's probably part of the reason I ended up in computer science.

Moving on... I've been back in the LA area for almost a month now, and honestly, I wonder why I ever left. Of course, I'm glad I went to Penn, because I met some pretty cool people and learned some pretty cool things, but there's something about southern Califoria that just feels like home: the sunshine, the warmth, the familiar sight of palm trees against the sky. The food is also top-notch- in the short weeks I've been home, I've enjoyed all-you-can-eat Korean barbeque, kimchi jigae and japchae, sushi and sashimi, ramen, poke, $1 tacos from the truck by the gas station, and oodles and oodles of boba. Of course, being home also means lots of kale, quinoa, and all the exotic fruits and greens rarely- if ever- found in Penn dining halls.

It's true that most of my time here has been spent eating/exploring new things to eat, but to be fair, I've tried to get some work done on the side, too. This is my third week as an intern at AuriQ Systems, a software company based in nearby Pasadena. I've had a few different part-time jobs, but this is my first full-time one, and it definitely takes getting used to. Things are going well so far; more updates to come! For now, I'm just happy to be home, with family, friends, and food. ☺

about me

Hi! I'm a sophomore studying computer science at the University of Pennsylvania. I grew up in Southern California, about an hour from the beach and 20 minutes from everywhere else. I love to explore new places, new foods, and new technologies, and this is where i write about all three.
